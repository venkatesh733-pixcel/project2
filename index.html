The tiger is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. An apex predator, it primarily preys on ungulates, such as deer and wild boar.

Wikipedia

Tiger's Speciality

  1. Tigers have soft toe pads which help them walk silently through their habitat.
  2. A tiger will typically travel 6-12 miles during a night's hunting.
  3. During the day, a tiger's stripes can act as camouflage, allowing it to blend in with its surroundings for stalking and ambushing its prey.

Amazing Facts About Tigers You May Not Know

  • Tigers are the largest amongst other wild cats.
  • A punch from a Tiger may kill you.
  • Tigers love to swim and play in the water.
  • Tigers can sprint at over 60 kilometre/hour.
  • Tigers can imitate the call of other animals.
